Black Belts

The word karate comes from the Japanese words, "empty hands". Karate originated in the Okinawan islands. It used by farmers to defend themselves from the cruel samurai. The weapons used in karate originated from farm tools. Since commoners were prohibited from having weapons, they modified simple objects for protection. The martial arts style spread throughout Asia, and in recent times reached the United States. The first karate dojo was opened in America in 1945.
